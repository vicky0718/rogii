Wellbore Geology Competition Understanding And Problem Decomposition
ROGII – Wellbore Geology Prediction
Comprehensive interpretation of the provided competition description, slides, diagrams, and problem mechanics.
1. Core Problem Definition
The competition objective is:
Predict the geological position of a horizontal wellbore after a certain point called the Prediction Start (PS).
The target variable is:
TVT = True Vertical Thickness / geological vertical positioning metric.
The prediction is evaluated using:
RMSE between predicted TVT and manually interpreted TVT.
Current leaderboard benchmark mentioned:
RMSE ≈ 8.966
Lower RMSE is better.
2. Mathematical Objective
Evaluation metric:
RMSE = sqrt( (1/n) * Σ(yi - yhat_i)^2 )
Implications:
Large prediction errors are penalized quadratically.
A few badly predicted regions can significantly worsen leaderboard score.
Continuity and stability matter.
Geological discontinuities or sudden wrong directional shifts are dangerous.
Local alignment quality matters more than just average trend.
This means:
Small systematic drift over long intervals is harmful.
Abrupt geological misalignment is extremely harmful.
Sequence consistency matters.
3. Fundamental Geological Interpretation
This is NOT a generic tabular regression problem.
The problem is fundamentally:
geological sequence matching
subsurface trajectory interpretation
depth correlation
signal alignment

sequential trajectory inference
The model must infer where the horizontal well sits inside geological layers.
The geology itself is not directly observed.
Instead, we infer geological position indirectly using:
Gamma Ray signatures
spatial trajectory
neighboring geological structures
typewell correlation
already-known TVT before PS
